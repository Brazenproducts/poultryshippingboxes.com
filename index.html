Why Plastic for Poultry Shipping?

Frozen poultry export is one of the most demanding cold-chain applications in the food industry. Birds go directly from the processing line into a blast freezer operating at −30 °C or colder, then into refrigerated containers for shipping — sometimes weeks at sea. The packaging has to survive all of it without warping, delaminating, or absorbing moisture that would harbour bacteria or cause label failure at the destination.

Wax-coated cardboard has been the standard for decades, but it has real limitations that become expensive at scale. The wax coating is applied to resist brief moisture exposure, not sustained freezing and thawing. In blast freezers, the wax can crack and the board beneath it absorbs condensation during the thaw phase. Wet boards collapse under stack pressure and create drainage issues on the processing floor. And every box is single use — at scale that is a significant recurring cost and waste stream.

PP plastic poultry shipping boxes eliminate these failure modes. Polypropylene remains flexible and structurally sound at temperatures as low as −30 °C, the box floor and walls are impermeable to water, and the boxes are fully washable between trips. A well-maintained fleet of plastic boxes typically pays back its capital cost versus cardboard within 12 to 18 months.

Blast Freeze Performance

The critical test for any poultry shipping container is what happens in the blast freezer. Temperatures of −30 to −40 °C combined with high airflow create thermal shock conditions that expose any weakness in the material. PP — particularly copolymer PP with impact modifiers — handles this well. The material does not become brittle at these temperatures, the molded corners remain sharp and strong, and the stacking feet retain their dimensional accuracy so boxes stack flush under load.

Ventilation is equally important. Birds freeze faster and more uniformly when air can circulate around the product. Our boxes are available with perforated side panels or slot venting in configurations validated for standard blast tunnel geometries. This matters for compliance with core-temperature protocols in markets like the EU, US, and the Gulf.

Export Compliance

Plastic packaging is ISPM 15 exempt by definition — it does not require heat treatment, fumigation, or phytosanitary certificates the way solid wood pallets and packing material do. For markets that are strict on phytosanitary documentation (EU, Australia, New Zealand, Japan), using plastic boxes throughout the cold chain simplifies paperwork and reduces the risk of consignment holds at the port.

All Elipacko food-grade boxes are manufactured from virgin or food-approved recycled PP or HDPE and carry the relevant food-contact declarations for FDA 21 CFR as applicable. Full traceability documentation is available on request for HACCP and BRC audits.

Custom Print and Branding

Poultry exporters typically ship to multiple markets with different language requirements, different retailer branding specifications, and different regulatory labelling. Plastic boxes can be custom-molded with embossed logos and printed with pad print, screen print, or in-mold labelling depending on volume and durability requirements. This makes it practical to maintain branded box fleets for key customers without the lead time and minimum order constraints of custom cardboard print runs.

Washing and Logistics

Plastic poultry shipping boxes are fully compatible with industrial tunnel washers operating at 60–85 °C with caustic detergent. Most processing plants already run wash lines for plastic bins and trays — poultry shipping boxes pass through the same equipment. Clean boxes nest or stack for return logistics: nested empty boxes occupy roughly 30% of the volume of stacked full boxes, reducing return freight cost significantly compared with flat-packing cardboard waste.

Return pooling schemes — where boxes are collected at the destination market and returned to the exporter — are well established in fresh poultry supply chains. For exporters running high volumes to a fixed customer base, the economics are compelling: a box that costs 8–12× a cardboard equivalent but lasts 50+ trips has a per-trip cost well below single-use packaging once logistics are accounted for.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can these boxes go through a chlorine wash?

Yes. PP and HDPE are chemically resistant to the chlorine concentrations used in food-industry sanitation (typically 200–500 ppm NaOCl). Prolonged exposure to high concentrations above 1000 ppm may cause minor surface dulling over many cycles but does not affect structural integrity.

What is the minimum order quantity?

Standard stock sizes are available ex-warehouse with no minimum. Custom dimensions or custom print require a tooling investment and typically a minimum run of 500–1000 units depending on size. Contact Elipacko for a project-specific quote.

Are the boxes suitable for fresh (non-frozen) poultry too?

Yes. The same boxes are used on fresh poultry lines running at +2 to +4 °C. The ventilation options suit fresh lines where airflow and drainage are important. For fresh applications, the solid-floor version with drainage slots is typically preferred.

Do you supply lids?

Yes — snap-fit and clip lids are available for all standard sizes. Lids are particularly important for IQF product and for export shipments where customs inspection may require re-sealing of the box.
