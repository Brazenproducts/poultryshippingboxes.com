Why PP Outperforms Cardboard in the Poultry Cold Chain

Wax-coated cardboard has been the default poultry export box for 40 years. It performs acceptably in a single-trip application — but it has two failure modes that cause real commercial damage in modern cold chains.

Moisture Collapse

Wax-coated cardboard absorbs moisture at every splice, fold, and score line — areas where the wax coating is disrupted. In a busy cold store or during transit in a refrigerated container with high humidity, wax boxes can lose 30–40% of their compressive strength. Stack failures on pallets during sea freight are almost always wax-box-related.

Dimension Inconsistency

Cardboard boxes vary by up to 5 mm in height as they absorb or release moisture. This causes uneven pallet stacks that shift during ocean freight and can trigger tipping of the entire pallet in rough seas. Plastic shipping boxes maintain ±3 mm dimensional tolerance in any humidity condition — the pallet stack is as level on arrival as when it left the plant.

Reuse Economics

For closed-loop export operations — where boxes are recovered from the importer and returned — PP shipping boxes complete 50–100+ trips versus a single trip for cardboard. The payback period at scale is typically 12–18 months. Contact Elipacko for a cost comparison model for your specific volume.

Specifying Poultry Shipping Boxes for Your Export Line

Single-Trip vs Reusable

The choice between single-trip and reusable PP poultry shipping boxes depends on your distribution model. If boxes leave your facility and are not recovered — typical for retail distribution and spot-export markets — a lighter-spec single-trip PP box is the most economical choice. If you operate a closed loop with a regular importer who returns boxes, the heavier reusable spec pays back faster.

Elipacko can supply both specifications from the same tooling by adjusting wall thickness. The difference in unit cost is typically 20–30%, and the structural difference is meaningful: reusable spec maintains its compressive strength through 100+ trip cycles, while single-trip spec is optimised for lowest cost at one or two uses.

Lid Sealing for Frozen Export

For frozen product on long sea voyages, a press-fit or snap-fit lid is strongly recommended. The lid maintains temperature uniformity within the box stack, prevents contamination from condensation dripping from refrigerated container ceilings, and provides a printable surface for additional product information. Specify lid type when requesting a quote from Elipacko.
